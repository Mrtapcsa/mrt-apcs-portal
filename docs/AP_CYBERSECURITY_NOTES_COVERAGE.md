# AP Cybersecurity Notes Coverage

This file documents the Phase 1 AP Cybersecurity notes now populated in the portal. The notes are written as original summaries and use official College Board AP Cybersecurity information as the curriculum anchor.

## Source Standard

- Primary source: College Board AP Cybersecurity course overview, course audit requirements, and exam information.
- Supporting sources: NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0, NIST CSRC Glossary, NIST Digital Identity Guidelines, NIST NICE Framework, CISA Secure Our World resources, and OWASP Top 10 or OWASP Cheat Sheet Series where appropriate.
- Excluded: unsourced notes, outdated AP CSA or AP CSP curriculum structures, copied CED passages, and the Firebase configuration shared during Option A.

## Student Note Structure

Every AP Cybersecurity lesson includes:

- Lesson title
- Learning objectives
- Key vocabulary
- Essential concepts
- Step-by-step explanation
- Worked example
- Code, log, policy, permission, or artifact example where useful
- Common mistakes
- Exam tips
- Summary
- Practice questions
- References

## AP Skill Alignment

- Analyze Risk: students identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, attack methods, likelihood, impact, and evidence.
- Mitigate Risk: students select protective controls that directly reduce the stated risk.
- Detect Attacks: students use logs, permissions, firewall rules, policy excerpts, alerts, and artifacts to support conclusions.
- Collaborate: students practice clear roles, documentation, responsible AI use, and professional norms where appropriate.
